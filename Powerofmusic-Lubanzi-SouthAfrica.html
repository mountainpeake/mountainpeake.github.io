The Children of Lubanzi

January 14, 2018

When six solo travelers rent a van for two weeks and travel the Wild Coast of South Africa.

A journey of unexpected friendships and everlasting memories.

The sun occasionally peeked through the clouds but it was hard to ignore the dark scattered showers looming over the ocean. Undeterred, we made our way towards the grassy hills. Smooth latin jazz filled the air as Vince flipped through his music. He seemingly always found just the right track for every moment. Back in Hamburg he is a techno DJ, but while traveling through the Wild Coast, he was our travel DJ.

The children playing in the water quickly took notice of us as we passed by. This was one of our first stops along the Wild Coast, a far cry from the bustling tourist hub that is the Garden Route. The children flocked from the water one by one curious to see where we were going. Despite none of us speaking Zulu, we managed to rely on hand gestures and smiles to communicate.

The children began to join Vince in a dance. One by one they all wanted to join in. When we finally reached the top of the hill, the rain had caught up to us. We moved our way to the edge of the hill. Gazing out onto the vast blue ocean ahead, we started dancing all together as the rain poured down.

I've known for some time that music can create powerful connections. But to predict the transformative experience that was about to unfold over the course of that day would've been impossible.

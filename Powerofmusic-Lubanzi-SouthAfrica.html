The Children of Lubanzi

January 24, 2019

When six solo travelers rent a van for two weeks and travel the Wild Coast of South Africa.

A journey of unexpected friendships and everlasting memories.

The sun occasionally peeked through the clouds but it was hard to ignore the dark scattered showers looming over the ocean. Undeterred, we made our way towards the grassy hills. This was one of our first stops along the Wild Coast and a far cry from the busy Garden Route. It's safe to say that the Wild Coast is worthy of being truly wild.

Vince flipped through his music, eventually settling on smooth latin vibes. He seemingly always found just the right track for every moment. Back in Hamburg he is a techno DJ, but while traveling through the Wild Coast, he was our travel DJ. The children playing in the water quickly took notice of us as we passed by. One by one they flocked from the water, curious to see what we were up to.

Despite none of us speaking Zulu, we managed to rely on hand gestures and smiles to communicate. The children began to join Vince in a dance. One by one they all wanted to join in. Once finally reaching the top of the hill, the rain had caught up to us. We moved our way to the edge of the hill gazing out onto the vast blue ocean ahead. We all started dancing together as the rain poured down.

I've known for some time that music can create powerful connections. However its ability to break down language barriers, connect generations and share cultures was a truly memorable experience. And the day had just started, none of us would've ever been able to predict how the rest of the day was about to unfold.
